About Women in STEM

Understanding the historical context and current landscape for women in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics

Breaking Barriers

Despite facing significant historical barriers and ongoing challenges, women have made remarkable contributions to STEM fields throughout history. From Marie Curie's groundbreaking work in radioactivity to Katherine Johnson's critical calculations for NASA space missions, women have shaped our understanding of the world and advanced innovation.

Today, organizations and institutions worldwide are working to increase female representation in STEM fields, recognizing that diversity drives innovation and scientific progress.

Our Mission

To inspire, educate, and support the next generation of women leaders in STEM fields through awareness, mentorship, and resource sharing.

STEM Calendar

Celebrating women who have made remarkable contributions to science, technology, engineering, and mathematics

Katherine Johnson

Mathematician

Katherine Johnson was a mathematician whose calculations of orbital mechanics as a NASA employee were critical to the success of the first U.S. crewed spaceflights.

Grace Hopper

Computer Scientist

Grace Hopper was an American computer scientist and US Navy rear admiral. She was a pioneer of computer programming who invented one of the first compiler tools.

Rosalind Franklin

Chemist & X-ray Crystallographer

Rosalind Franklin's work was central to understanding the molecular structures of DNA, RNA, viruses, coal, and graphite.
